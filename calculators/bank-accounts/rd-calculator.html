What is a Recurring Deposit (RD)?

A Recurring Deposit (RD) is a special kind of Term Deposit offered by banks in India. It helps people with regular incomes to deposit a fixed amount every month into their RD account and earn interest at the rate applicable to Fixed Deposits.

Think of it as an "FD in installments." Instead of investing a lump sum of ₹1 Lakh at once (which many people don't have), you invest ₹5,000 every month for 20 months.

RD vs. SIP: The Ultimate Battle

Both RD and SIP involve investing a fixed amount monthly. But they are vastly different products.

How Banks Calculate RD Interest

The formula for RD calculation is surprisingly complex. Banks use the Quarterly Compounding formula on each monthly installment.

This means your 1st installment earns interest for 12 months, your 2nd installment earns for 11 months, and so on. Since the tenure for each installment is different, the compounding effect varies for each ₹5,000 you deposit.

Rules You Must Know

1. Penalty for Missed Payments

Unlike SIPs where you can skip a month without penalty, banks charge a fine if you miss an RD installment. Usually, it is ₹1.50 for every ₹100 per month of delay. If you miss 4 consecutive installments, the bank may close your RD account prematurely.

2. TDS on RD Interest

Earlier, TDS was not applicable on RDs. However, since 2015, RD interest is taxable just like FD interest.
If your total interest income exceeds ₹40,000/year, the bank will deduct 10% TDS. Submit Form 15G/15H to avoid this if your total income is below the taxable limit.

3. Premature Closure

You can close your RD before the tenure ends, but banks deduct a 1% penalty from the interest rate applicable for the period the deposit remained with the bank.
