BMI Categories Explained: What the Numbers Mean

Educational health guide

Body Mass Index (BMI) is a simple calculation used to assess body weight relative to height. It is commonly used as a screening tool.

What Is BMI?

BMI is calculated by dividing a person's weight by the square of their height. It provides a general indication of weight status.

BMI Categories

  • Underweight: Below 18.5
  • Normal weight: 18.5 – 24.9
  • Overweight: 25 – 29.9
  • Obese: 30 and above

Limitations of BMI

BMI does not measure body fat directly and may not apply equally to athletes, elderly individuals, or children.
