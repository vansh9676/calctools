How EMI Is Calculated (With Formula & Practical Example)

Educational guide · Updated regularly

Equated Monthly Installment (EMI) is the fixed amount you pay every month towards repaying a loan. Whether it is a home loan, car loan, or personal loan, understanding how EMI is calculated helps you make better borrowing decisions.

What Is EMI?

EMI consists of two components: the principal amount and the interest. In the early years of a loan, a larger portion goes towards interest, while later payments reduce the principal more significantly.

The EMI Formula

The standard EMI formula used by banks is:

EMI = [P × R × (1 + R)N] ÷ [(1 + R)N − 1]

  • P = Loan principal
  • R = Monthly interest rate (annual rate ÷ 12)
  • N = Loan tenure in months

Step-by-Step EMI Example

Suppose you take a loan of ₹5,00,000 at an annual interest rate of 10% for 5 years (60 months).

  • Principal (P): ₹5,00,000
  • Monthly Interest (R): 10% ÷ 12 = 0.00833
  • Tenure (N): 60 months

Using the EMI formula, your monthly EMI comes to approximately ₹10,624.

How Interest Rate Affects EMI

Even a small change in interest rate can significantly impact your EMI. Higher interest rates increase your monthly burden and total interest paid.
