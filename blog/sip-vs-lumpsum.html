SIP vs Lump Sum: Which Investment Method Is Better?

Educational guide · Beginner friendly

SIP (Systematic Investment Plan) and Lump Sum are two popular ways of investing in mutual funds. Both approaches have advantages depending on financial goals and risk tolerance.

What Is SIP?

SIP allows you to invest a fixed amount at regular intervals. It promotes disciplined investing and reduces market timing risk.

What Is Lump Sum Investment?

Lump sum investment involves investing a large amount at one time. Returns depend heavily on market timing.

Key Differences

  • SIP spreads risk over time
  • Lump sum benefits from market lows
  • SIP is suitable for salaried investors
  • Lump sum suits investors with surplus funds

Which Is Better?

There is no universal answer. SIP is generally safer for beginners, while lump sum may suit experienced investors.
