Financial Planning 101: The Basics

1. The 50-30-20 Rule of Budgeting

Financial planning doesn't have to be complicated. The gold standard for budgeting is the 50-30-20 Rule. It simplifies your monthly income allocation into three clear buckets:

  • 50% Needs: Essential expenses like Rent, EMI, Groceries, and Utilities. These are non-negotiable.
  • 30% Wants: Discretionary spending like Dining Out, Netflix, Travel, and Shopping. This is your "fun" money.
  • 20% Savings: The most crucial part. This goes into SIPs, PPF, and Emergency Funds. Use our SIP Calculator to see how this 20% grows.

2. Debt Management Strategy

Not all debt is bad. "Good Debt" (like a Home Loan) helps build an asset, while "Bad Debt" (like Credit Card dues) destroys wealth.

The key to managing a Home Loan is Prepayment. Banks structure loans so that you pay mostly interest in the first 10 years. By paying just one extra EMI per year, you can fight this interest trap and close a 20-year loan in just 15 years. Check the math on our Home Loan Calculator.
