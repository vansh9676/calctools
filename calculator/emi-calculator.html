About EMI Calculation

EMI (Equated Monthly Installment) is the fixed payment amount made by a borrower to a lender at a specified date each calendar month. EMIs are used to pay off both interest and principal each month, so that over a specified number of years, the loan is paid off in full.

What is EMI?

EMI stands for Equated Monthly Instalment. It is the fixed monthly amount you repay to the bank or lender for a home loan, car loan, or personal loan. EMI includes both loan principal and interest cost.

Frequently Asked Questions

1. Does EMI increase or decrease over time?

No. EMI remains fixed throughout tenure unless you restructure loan.

2. Can I reduce my EMI?

Yes. By increasing tenure or making part-payment.

3. Is GST applied on loan EMI?

No GST on loan principal or interest for personal borrowers.
