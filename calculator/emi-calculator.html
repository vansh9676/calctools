What is EMI?

EMI stands for Equated Monthly Installment. It is the fixed amount that a borrower pays every month to repay a loan. Each EMI consists of two parts: the principal portion and the interest portion.

In the early years of a loan, a larger part of the EMI goes towards interest. As the loan tenure progresses, the interest component reduces and the principal repayment increases.

How EMI is Calculated

EMI is calculated using a standard mathematical formula that considers the loan amount, interest rate, and loan tenure. Even a small change in interest rate or tenure can significantly impact the EMI and total interest paid.

EMI for Different Loan Types

EMI calculation works similarly for home loans, car loans, and personal loans. However, interest rates and tenures differ significantly. Home loans usually have longer tenures and lower interest rates, while personal loans have higher rates and shorter durations.

Should You Choose Longer or Shorter Tenure?

Longer tenure reduces EMI but increases total interest paid. Shorter tenure increases EMI but saves interest. Choosing the right tenure depends on your income stability and financial goals.
