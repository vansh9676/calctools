What Is BMI?

Body Mass Index (BMI) is a numerical value calculated using a person’s height and weight. It is widely used as a simple screening method to categorize individuals into different weight groups.

How BMI Is Calculated

BMI is calculated using the formula: BMI = weight (kg) ÷ height² (m²). This calculator converts height into meters and applies the formula automatically.

BMI Categories

  • Underweight: Below 18.5
  • Normal weight: 18.5 – 24.9
  • Overweight: 25 – 29.9
  • Obese: 30 and above

Example BMI Calculation

If a person weighs 70 kg and is 170 cm tall, their BMI would be approximately 24.2, which falls under the normal weight category.

Limitations of BMI

BMI does not directly measure body fat and may not accurately represent athletes, elderly individuals, or children. It should be used as a general guideline only.
